How to get groceries during lockdown

COVID-19 is disrupting India in an unprecedented manner. In order to deal with the CoronaVirus, with a country of over 1.3 billion people going into lock-down for a period of 38 days starting March 25th, 2020.During this lockdown It's extremely difficult to get grocery and other basic commodities. What's available is non-branded and much costlier.Prices of vegetables and fruits were already high and now rice, pulses and flour are also costlier." People has to pay almost 20% to 30% more for whatever goods are still availiable in limited quantities.First commodities became scarce due to panic-buying and hoarding, triggering a price rise. Prices then started skyrocketing as goods trucks remain halted, there are no fresh supplies and migrant labourers have been heading home in most parts of the country.

Long queues outside grocery stores, inflated prices, difficulty in getting important items -- these are some of the problems encountered by people in Delhi NCR .Despite authorities asking people not to step out and assuring that essential supplies will not be affected, people rushed out to stock daily need goods. The e-commerce platforms are also unable to deliver most basic commodities as they are out of supplies.other cities are also feeling the same pinch
